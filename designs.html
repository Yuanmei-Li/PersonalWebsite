Modular Droplet Generator

Last updated: 2025-05-07 Tags: DIY, low-cost, open-hardware

Overview

This modular droplet generator is based on the design of Harris's "A low cost, precise piezoelectric droplet on demand generator" (https://doi.org/10.1007/s00348-015-1950-6). I've made some improvements to make it more cost - effective and easier to use.

The cost of this design is extremely low. Excluding laboratory infrastructure like peristaltic pumps or easily borrowable items, other components cost less than $10 (assuming access to a 3D printer). Even without a 3D printer, it's not a big issue as I've replaced the delicate parts with inexpensive industrial products. It has a split - type design, which is easy to fabricate, assemble, modify, and move.

The main design feature is the use of various inexpensive industrial products in a modular way to replace the delicate structures of the original model. This ensures precise control of droplet size, similar to the original design principle. For detailed information, please refer to the design drawings and instructions.

Notes

If possible, it's recommended to use a stereolithography 3D printer. If using a filament - based 3D printer, set the fill rate to 100% and perform post - curing to prevent water leakage and ensure proper vibration transfer of the piezoelectric ceramic buzzer.

You can check the previous design versions on the Blog page, which had various issues. I'm currently testing oleophobic coatings to make liquid droplets roll in the lab. As there's no reliable super - oleophobic coating for silicone oil yet, I'm still experimenting and reading papers. There might be some potential here, but it's hard to tell for now.
