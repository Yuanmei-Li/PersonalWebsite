Walking Droplets

Millimeter-sized droplets can self-propel across a vertically vibrating fluid bath when the vibration amplitude is between the walking threshold (aw) and Faraday threshold (aF). This results from a delicate balance where:

  • Γ < Γw: Simple bouncing without horizontal motion
  • Γw ≤ Γ < ΓF: Stable walking state
  • Γ ≥ ΓF: Faraday wave instability dominates

Together with the super walking droplets generated at the superimposed frequency that can exhibit wonderful states such as the aggregation of multiple droplets, ultrafast walking, lattice structures, etc., the walking state exhibits remarkable quantum-like features.

Hydrodynamic Quantum Analogs

This macroscopic system recreates numerous quantum phenomena with striking fidelity:

  • Tunneling: Through potential barriers
  • Quantum corral: Circular confinement
  • Landau levels: In rotating frames
  • Hong-Ou-Mandel effect: Droplet "interference"
    • ...

The analog approach provides physical intuition for quantum mechanics while raising profound questions about the nature of wave-particle duality.

Pilot Wave Theory

The de Broglie-Bohm interpretation proposes that quantum particles are guided by a "pilot wave" that determines their trajectories. Key aspects:

  • Reintroduced at 1927 Solvay Conference but overshadowed
  • Revived by walking droplet experiments in 2005
  • Features non-local quantum potential
  • Provides deterministic alternative to Copenhagen interpretation

Our work explores how pre-existing wave fields could mediate non-local correlations, potentially explaining entanglement through synchronized dynamics.

Schematic diagram of a pilot wave guiding a droplet trajectory

Chaotic Synchronization

When multiple walking droplets interact through their shared wave field, remarkable synchronization emerges:

  • Phase-locked orbiting in circular confinement
  • Anti-phase oscillations in double-well potentials
  • Chaotic synchronization with sensitive dependence
  • Analogies to quantum coherence phenomena

Current research focuses on quantifying these effects and exploring connections to quantum entanglement analogs.
